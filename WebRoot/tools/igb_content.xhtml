Integrated Genome Browser

Launch Integrated Genome Browser

The Integrated Genome Browser (IGB) is an interactive genome browser tool that runs on your local computer rather than within a Web site as with other genome browser tools.

IGB is different from other tools in that it lets you open, explore, visualize, and analyze your RNA-Seq, ChIP-Seq, epigenetics, tiling array, and other large-scale data sets, displaying your data alongside publicly available data sets, including gene models and the reference sequence itself.

Using IGB's QuickLoad system, you can also use IGB to share data with collaborators and members of the community.

To download and launch IGB, click one of the buttons below. Choose a button based on how much computer memory (RAM) you want IGB to use.

Requirements: You must have Java 1.6 or better to use IGB and you must have enough RAM to use the memory option you download.

Once you launch IGB, choose species Arabidopsis thaliana and the version of the genome you would like to view. TAIR annotated gene models should load automatically. Right-click a gene model and choose the link labeled arabidopsis.org to view he corresponding Web page at TAIR.

Note: If you have trouble launching IGB, please consult the on troubleshooting IGB.

About IGB

IGB receives support from National Science Foundation Award from the Arabidopsis 2010 program. A community of open source developers contribute to IGB, including researchers from diverse areas, including plant genomics.

IGB was first developed at which released IGB as open source software in 2004. Starting in 2008, the in the at has led development of IGB, with contributions from scientists and developers all over the world.

Other contributors have included IGB founder Gregg Helt, now of Ed Erwin of Affymetrix, David Nix and Toni DiSera of at the Michael Lawrence and Lance Frohman of Research and Early Development, and Ido Tamir of the in Vienna, Austria.
